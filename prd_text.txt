03 — World-Class UI/UX Principles

This site must meet the standard of a Pentagram-designed product. These are not aesthetic preferences — they are engineering constraints that govern every component, every spacing decision, every interaction.

3.1 The Ten Laws of Great Design — Applied to Zilhak

Law 1 — Clarity Over Cleverness (Dieter Rams + Jony Ive)

Every screen must communicate ONE thing immediately. If a visitor needs to 'figure out' what Zilhak does, the design has failed. The headline must state exactly what Zilhak does in 5–7 words. Jony Ive: 'It's very easy to be different, but very difficult to be better.'

IMPLEMENTATION RULE: Hero headline must be decided and locked BEFORE any code is written. The headline is the most important element on the site — more important than the Three.js animation behind it.

Law 2 — Invisible Design (Jared Spool)

'Good design, when done well, should be invisible.' Users should never notice the interface — they should only experience the content and the brand. Every interaction that draws attention to itself (loading spinner, janky animation, layout shift) is a failure.

Law 3 — Visual Hierarchy as Communication (Apple HIG)

Every page must have a clear visual hierarchy: what the eye sees first (size, weight, contrast), what it sees second, what it scans last. Apple's HIG mandates: Primary element → Secondary element → Tertiary detail. Every component must follow this pattern.

Law 4 — Reduce Cognitive Load (Jakob Nielsen)

'Clear content, simple navigation and answers to customer questions have the biggest impact on business value. Advanced technology matters much less.' — Jakob Nielsen, Nielsen Norman Group. The navigation must be maximum 6 items. Each page must answer 1 question. Services page answers: 'What exactly do you build?'. Work page answers: 'What have you already built and for whom?'.

Law 5 — Typography IS the Interface

On a dark tech site, typography carries 80% of the personality. Font pairing rules: ONE display font (personality), ONE body font (readability), ONE monospace font (technical authority). These three fonts, used consistently, create the entire character of the brand.

Law 6 — Motion Must Have Meaning (Apple HIG + WWDC25)

Apple's 2025 design update (Liquid Glass) establishes: animations must reinforce content relationships, not decorate them. Every GSAP animation must answer: 'Why is this element moving? What does the motion communicate?' Random scroll animations for decoration are the enemy of quality.

Law 7 — Mobile-First is Non-Negotiable

57% of Pakistani internet users are on mobile (PTA 2025 data). The site must be designed mobile-first — every component designed for a 375px screen first, then expanded to desktop. Not the reverse. Mobile is not a 'version' — it IS the primary experience.

Law 8 — Performance is a Design Choice

A site that loads in 4 seconds is a poorly designed site. Lighthouse 90+ is a design requirement, not a developer concern. Every animation, every Three.js particle, every custom font must be justified against its performance cost.

Law 9 — Accessibility is Not Optional

WCAG 2.1 AA compliance minimum. All text must meet 4.5:1 contrast ratio on dark backgrounds. All interactive elements must have visible focus states. All images must have alt text. Custom cursor must degrade gracefully on touch devices. This isn't charity — it expands your audience and improves SEO.

Law 10 — Specificity Builds Trust

'We build AI solutions' builds zero trust. 'We built a WhatsApp chatbot for a Lahore retail chain that handled 3,000 customer queries/month' builds enormous trust. Every project card, every service description, every testimonial must be as SPECIFIC as possible. Vague = unbelievable.

04 — Zilhak Logo System — Three Concepts

After deep research into Pentagram, Wolff Olins, Ramotion, Collins, IBM, Vercel, and OpenAI brand identities, three distinct logo concepts were developed. All three share the Z identity — the mark carries your name's identity. One is the master logo; the others are contextual variants of the same brand system.



CONCEPT A — "SIGNAL"  —  Electric Precision

USE CASE: Investor decks · Microsoft pitch · LinkedIn banner · Email signature

Philosophy: IBM meets DeepMind. The Z is constructed from raw signal lines with glowing endpoint nodes — like a circuit trace or waveform. Zero decoration. Top bar (blue) + diagonal slash + bottom bar (teal) connected by glowing nodes. Extension dashes trail outward: "signal beyond the mark." This tells investors: we are technical, precise, and serious. Colors: #0A84FF → #00C2FF → #00E5C3. WHEN TO USE: Every investor-facing document, pitch deck cover, LinkedIn profile, business card front.



CONCEPT B — "AXIS"  —  Geometric Sovereignty

USE CASE: Master logo · Business cards · Legal documents · FBR filings · Letterhead

Philosophy: McKinsey meets Palantir. A perfect diamond with the Z carved as negative space inside — the same hidden-meaning technique FedEx used with their famous arrow and Carrefour with their hidden C. The mark rewards second glances. Diamond vertices have white dot accents. Wordmark uses heavy weight (ZILHAK) + ultralight contrast (GLOBAL ASSOCIATES) + diamond center hairline rule. This is the 20-year logo — the one that still works when Zilhak is a unicorn. WHEN TO USE: This is the PRIMARY master logo for all official, permanent, and legal contexts.



CONCEPT C — "NEXUS"  —  Kinetic Intelligence

USE CASE: Website hero · App icon · Social media · AI product marketing · Animations

Philosophy: Vercel meets OpenAI. Four glowing network nodes (purple → gold) connected by gradient paths forming the Z shape. Each node is labeled: BUILD · AUTOMATE · SCALE · INNOVATE — your four service pillars. The wordmark splits: Z is heavy purple (glowing), ILHAK is ultralight white — making Z the visual anchor. This logo is natively animation-ready: nodes can pulse, paths can animate along their gradients, the whole mark can breathe on the website hero. WHEN TO USE: Website, app store icon, social media profiles, AI product contexts, presentations.
